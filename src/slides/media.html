Are our media accessible?

When we develop for our stylesheet, we almost always have a screen in mind. A laptop screen, or a smartphone screen. Maybe even a tablet screen. But always a screen.

But it hasn't been always like this. On the contrary, one of the first media we've used to access information didn't involve a screen at all. It didn't even involve an input device, RAM or a CPU. It was just paper. Printed paper. And even before, written paper. And before that, written stone or wax.

Of course, CSS didn't even exist back then. And neither did HTML. But that doesn't mean that putting things on paper is a thing of the past. After all, it was still the main information exchange medium when CSS was conceived, so it's only natural that it's been the first medium to be targeted by special styling after the screen.
