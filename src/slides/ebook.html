Chapter 1

Are our media accessible?

It was a dark and stormy night, and the room, dimly lit by the computer screen, was suddenly flooded by the brief flash of a lightning. When we develop for our stylesheet, we almost always have a screen in mind. A laptop screen, or a smartphone screen. Maybe even a tablet screen. But always a screen. The thunder finally came: the storm was close.

“But it hasn't been always like this,” thought Alice. On the contrary, one of the first media we've used to access information didn't involve a screen at all. It didn't even involve an input device, RAM or a CPU. It was just paper. Printed paper. And even before, written paper. And before that, written stone or wax.
